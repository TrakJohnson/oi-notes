3 1958 → 1969: A memorial conflict appeased or hidden ? The "de Gaulle" years

  • Dramatic André Malraux speech, "Entre ici Jean Moulin"
  • Even the communists are happy (Moulin was rather left, despite serving De Gaulle)

3.2 "Résistancialisme" as a solution to memorial conflict ?

France represented in the "Grande Vadrouille": no collaborators, all frenchmen work together against the germans 1966: Movie "Paris brûle-t-il ?" → liberation of Paris by Gaullists and Communists 1969: "L'armée des ombres" → glorification of the FFI Asterix reflects the mindset - tiny force resisting against the "envahisseur"

3.3 Only a temporary solution

  • Picture for the movie poster shows a french gendarme guarding a camp → censored by the state

4 1969 → Today: The upheaval of a myth, and new challenges

1970: De Gaulle dies, archives are reopened

4.1 "Vichy, un passé qui ne passe pas" - Henri Rousso

4.1.1 Another look on Vichy

A new, post-war generation 1969: "Chagrin et Pitié", movie with witnesses of french collaboration

  • Not allowed to be viewed on public television until 1981

1973: Paxton, "La France et Vichy"

  • Concludes that Vichy collaboration has amplified the German initiatives → many collaborators, Vichy was autonomous
    • Excuse was that they were trying to "please" the Germans

1981: Paxton, "Vichy et les Juifs"

  • Used archives, admits that there was more resistance than he thought, and earlier than planned
    • However collaboration was worse than he thought
    • Pétain claims that he had "le couteau sous la gorge", yet Paxton finds no evidence of German initiative for the 1940 "statut des juifs"

4.1.2 Reaction from Vichyists and far-right: negationism

1978: Pellepoix, one of Petain's ministers, claims that only lice were killed in concentration camps Faurisson: negationism, "rumeur d'Auschwitz", minimizes the numbers

4.1.3 Fight against negationism

  • 1990 Gayssot Law
  • Stimulates research of nazis and collaborators

4.2 Jewish memory awakens

1961: Eichmann kidnapped and judged 1964: Crimes against humanity are declared imprescriptible 1970: Klarsfeld couple searches for collaborators & victims

  • Creates memorials
  • Leads to trials of:
    • Paul Touvier's, head of the milice
    • Bousquet, police and rafle of Vel d'Hiv
    • Klaus Barbie (Gestapo in Lyon)
    • Maurice Papon

1985 Film Shoah

4.3 Responsibility of the state

Miterrand (1981-1995):

  • The french government has no responsability
  • The Vichy government was illegitimate → Vichy ≠ République
    • The république has no excuses to make
  • "Il n'y a pas de controverses": Vichy is not France

Chirac (1995 - 2007)

  • Rupture with the other presidents
  • France is responsible: he admits collaboration of the state
    • Yet still "répond aux exigences" → still passive Etat Française vs truth of an active Pétain
  • "La France accomplissait l'irréparable": it is France and not "l'état français"
    • France has a debt
