How have the role and impact of the media influenced politics and public opinion in France ?

Examples used: Dreyfus & 1934 Dates used:

  • 1881: law for free press
  • 1898: Zola's J'accuse
  • 1934: Year of parlimentary crisis

Data used:

  • 30% of people initially for Dreyfus
  • 300 000 max tirages

1 Introduction

  • Today we are surrounded with media, Internet age
  • Media's original intent was to inform people about political and social events/change
    • However impact has changed
  • Thesis: Media has first become a driver and divider of political opinion in times of crises and scandals; it then evolved to become the driver of the events it described, and has caused political change.

2 Media as a driver of political opinion - Dreyfus

2.1 Origins of this role

  • End of the 19th century: press is the only form of media
  • Gained influence thanks to:
    • The end of governments control and censorship with the 1881 Law
    • Technological advancements meant that you could print very fast, and in great quantity
  • Result: press is excellent for spreading a political opinion
    • Even press that claimed to be neutral had to take sides
  • The press did not describe what it saw, but also led investigations
    • The Dreyfus affair simply wouldn't have existed without the role of the press

2.2 Press shapes opinions

  • Headlines accused the other party, or glorified their own → people cannot avoid taking sides
  • Massive number of prints (up to 300 000 of a single newspaper during the Dreyfus affair)
    • The omnipresence of the press, and therefore of opinions, split France in two
    • Unequal split: only 30% supported Dreyfus initially
      • Zola's "J'accuse" had a significant role in turning the tide, or at least bringing Dreyfus back into the game - as he had already been "judged" and imprisoned in Guyana at the time

2.3 Press impacts political decisions

  • Press shapes public opinion which exerts heavy pressure upon political decisions
  • Thanks to the press that the trial became an Affair
    • Example of Dreyfus' brother publishing fake stories in order to keep Dreyfus in the media and not have the public opinion forget about it
  • Zola's 1898 "J'accuse" changed opinions, but it was before anything a frontal assault on the govt (cited names of what he called traitors, which were within the Army)
    • Forced the govt to put Zola on trial, thus reigniting public opinion
  • Conclusion: press exerts considerable power on govt, and forces the reopening of the case

3 Media as a political actor - 1934 Crisis

3.1 Press calls to action

  • Press was the leader of the protests
    • Other causes: 1929 crises with economic and political instability
    • Press organized the public hate into protests
    • Headlines were not simply denouncing or criticizing ⇒ they were calling for revolt
  • Example: Mauras's Action Française calls for protests in front of Parliament
    • Denounced by left-wing newspapers who called for the own protests: "le coupe de force fasciste ne passera pas"
  • Media leads to success of front populaire
  • Beyond shaping of opinion and into concrete action

3.2 Opinion and facts

  • Opinions shaped the "facts" that they retold
    • Deformation of the original role of the press, which is to inform readers.
  • Canard Enchaîné mixes facts and opinions in its satirical headlines (about Stavisky for example)
  • Different versions:
    • Left newspapers (L'Humanité, le Populaire) claim 39 deaths, with policemen
    • Right newspapers say 50+ deaths, and thousands of wounded: "Paris baigne dans le sang"
    • Impression of reading factual data: even more impact, we don't know it's opinion and take it as the truth
  • Line between fact and opinion is blurred → media's power

3.3 Personal attacks

  • Media can exert pressure against or for individuals
  • During the Dreyfus affair already, pressure for and against Dreyfus himself
  • Salengro affair: "Premier ministre" pushed to suicide after defamation campaign by the right "Gringoire"
    • Attacking him and investigating his private life ⇒ committed suicide
  • Lead to the phrase "le fascisme assassin"
