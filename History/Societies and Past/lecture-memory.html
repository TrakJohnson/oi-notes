1 Historical Background

Petain becomes Prime Minister on June 15th, 1940

  • June 22nd, armistice

Resistance: secret initiatives of individuals, very low % of pop (~2.5%).

  • People's priority: survival, poverty, hunger, separation
  • Sparse groups
  • Jean Moulin: partly unifying, giving legitimacy to de Gaulle

1943 STO, 1944: Allied landing: brought more people to resistants

  • End war on the right side of history

  • Members of the German division found and judged for crimes against humanity
  • But 1/3 are alsacians, "war victims" or "war criminals"
  • Need to give a "non-lieu"

1.5m POW, STO workers, Deportees

  • we are all heroes, not victims .. ?

2.2 Fragmented Memory

2.2.1 Generational Revolt, Memory in Crisis

1968: the young people haven't lived through the war, want to be down with Gaullism and Communism

"Chagrin et la Pitié": reveals what random people did

  • Breaks the Gaullist myth

Robert Aron: Histoire de Vichy, says Pétain couldn't do anything Robert Paxton: Vichy France, collaboration was initiated by the French, Laval (1973)

2.2.2 Obsessive memory, memory on trial

1970s: impossible to create a new consensus

1980:the courts can hunt down war criminals - the courts decide history

At Liberation, no one had been tried for their role in the deportation of Jews, but for other reasons (on the french side)

  • 1980s: seen as the central crime
  • Papon for example, works in govt, scandal then → taken to trial

Switches to obsession to what the french did to the Jews

  • Mitterand doesn't want to recognize that: Vichy was not France
  • Chirac though says "France committed an irreperable act towards the Jews"
    • Past must be repaired, symbolic reparation

Repair the past: accept what you've done, financial reparation (Pressure from the Jewish Congress, US Congress + Treasury)

  • Payed 400million euros to Fondation de la Shoah
