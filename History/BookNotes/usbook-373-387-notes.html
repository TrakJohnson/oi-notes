1 Age of Affluence

1.1 Economic Prosperity

1960 → 1970:

  • $170B growth of GNP
  • Total wages grew 10%

1.2 Material affluence

1950: First credit cards, symbols of the consumer society

1960:

  • 60% owned own homes
  • 75% owned cars
  • 90% owned a TV

Number of industrial workers falling - more office jobs

  • People turning much more to leisure
    • Sports on TV, advertising is everywhere
  • Growth of music (rock and roll), mass media etc

Example: opening of Disneyland in 1955: only for the middle class, leisure and consumerism

1.3 Unequal affluence

  • Flight to the suburbs caused by the construction of nice houses and the growth of the automobile industry
    • Created ghettos in the city centers
  • Despite propsertity, there were time of recession and unemployment affected the poor
  • Growth of oligopolies meant that opportunities for new buisnesses were reduced and corporations had a lot of power
    • Small farms went out of buisness for example

2 JFK's domestic policy

New Frontier

2.1 Giving hope

Intentions to continue the Fair Deal:

  • Get rid of poverty, help civil rights, etc
    • Robert Kennedy got MLK out of jail
  • Based on the young born from the baby boom

Gives a very good appearance

  • Very important-sounding inauguration speech
  • Surrounded with intellectuals, the White House became "Camelot"

2.2 Achievements

2.2.1 Tech & Military

  • Desire to go further: lots of space funding (20% increase in military and space spending - NASA budget doubled)
  • Unemployment fell down, through federal spending
  • Fought inflation by remaining strong facing companies such as US Steel who were trying to bypass govt. contract

2.2.2 Social measures

  • Became interested in the status of the poor in 1962, after reading The Other America
    • Continued the work of Truman and Eisenhower
    • Minimum wage was extended (as it wasn't universal)
    • Setup training for low-qualified people who lost their jobs because of automation

More success in the environment policies

  • First president to address these issues seriously

However, in practice, conclusion is mitigated

  • Housing Act benefited more to the property builders than the poor
  • Area Redevelopment act money was used to build luxury hotels
    • Argued that it would create jobs
  • No more success in healthcare or education
    • This would be done by Johnson later

This was because JFK was busy with foreign affairs

  • Cuba crisis, U2, etc
  • Conservative coalition in Congress
  • However there was economic growth

3 Great Society

3.1 What ?

  • Massive legislative program (60 pieces of legislation passed)
    • Implementing many of the social measures that had not been done under JFK
  • War on Poverty
  • Education
    • Tried to fund primary school dev. in poor regions
    • More success with the superior education (universities)
  • Welfare - medical care and aid
    • Helped the elderly and funded the medical care
  • Improve transportation and living conditions
  • Housing - funding for the poor / low loan houses
  • Environment: creation of national parks, protecting endangered species
  • Gave access to theaters, museums, etc to poorer people

3.2 Why ?

  • White flight and decline in blue collar jobs had caused massive poverty in the city centers
    • Regions that depended on mining for example were on the decline
  • The Other Society book had influenced JFK and revealed how hidden the poor American population was
  • Segregation etc.

3.3 Was it great ?

Great in practice, hard to implement

  • Federal money for schools was controlled by the states, which decided not to give it to the poorest schools

Public support for such liberal reforms didn't last long

  • Compared to expectations, it didn't succeed
  • Govt. underestimated the problem
    • Real change was much harder, as it had to challenge existing structures which were firmly established in the US
  • Done too much in a rush - several things required more thought and planning, which lead to waste of money in some areas
    • "Of course we made mistakes. We were doing things !"
  • War on Vietnam pumped the money - only 10% of war's money was spent on the Great Society
  • Despite all these efforts, they didn't manage to include everyone - favored equality of opportunity rather than equality of results

There were successes:

  • Families in poverty dropped from 40 M (1959) → 25M (1970)
  • African American salaries went up
  • Projection of the environment
  • Improved life expectancy
