1 Postwar America

1.1 Victory !

  • US's victory results in a surge in patriotism
    • People singing national anthem, "God bless America", etc
    • National frenzy

1.2 People on the move

  • Important population migration in wartime
    • People went north to work in factories and participate in the war effort
    • Old south → New York, Chicago, Philadelphia, Detroit + West Coast
      • Especially african americans (higher paying jobs)
      • They became permanent residents of the metropolis
  • War industry meant lots of middle class
    • Mass culture: More leisure + importance of media

1.3 Economic transformation

  • New Deal didn't work → solution is wartime industry
  • However lots of national debt + inflation (no consumer goods available during the war)
    • OPA (Office of Price Administration)
  • Redistribution of wage
    • Workers have more pay !
  • Lots of govt. intervention leads to sucess in eliminating the depression

1.4 A diverse society

  • When the war finished - US was still a very rural country
  • Soldiers rapidly came back
    • Although many were happy to ..
    • .. some never recovered from physical or psychological wounds
      • others questionned whether it was worth it to fight for the greedy, materialistic society they came back to
  • 25% of population is foreign in US
    • 10% are african americans (+ hispanics, chinese, etc)

1.5 The growth of big labour

  • Importance of trade unions
  • However liberal stance during the war opposed conservatives* of the post-war (have to fight the communists)
    • Purchasing power + wages fell with postwar inflation
    • Consequence: a lot of strikes in 1945 → resulted in prove

1.6 A religious people

Even though the US was becoming more secular: still very religious

  • 45 million protestants
  • 25 million catholics
  • 5 million jewish

ECUMENICALISM: The doctrine of the ecumenical movement that promotes cooperation and better understanding among different religious denominations.

  • That was not very present
  • Conservative groups were still very powerful
    • anti-catholic feeling
    • discrimination against jews

1.7 Women

  • Many entered the labour force during the war (6 million 1941 → 1945)
    • Became skilled, earned high wages, etc
    • Quit domestic jobs
    • However still discrimination: women earned 60% of men's wages for equivalent work
  • They also became head of the family
    • Even more responsability given that there were few places to take care of children
  • However, after the end of the war, they were forced out to be replaced by men
    • Automobile industry: 25% women in 1945; 7% in 1946
    • They were put back in "woman jobs" (mostly low-paying)
  • And no one cared - until the 1960s

1.8 African Americans

  • WW2: As much segregation in the army as at home
    • However there was the promise of getting education and a better job after the war
    • Conditions were still better than WW1
  • NAACP grew from 50 000 (1940) → 500 000 (1945) people
  • More employment opportunities
    • Philip Randolph - african american labor leader - threatened a public march
    • FDR in exchange made the FEPC (Fair Employment Practices Commitee) which attempted to prevent discrimination at the workplace
      • More symbolic than useful

Exile from the South to the North

  • voting rights are in the north
    • they form an important voting mass
  • however society didn't change (the mindset is mostly the same)
  • June 1943: 3 days of riot, 25 african american and 9 whites dead
  • Black protests in the North were more common, and more effective

War gave opportunities

  • GI Bill: allowed GIs to get education, fund their buisness, etc.
  • Gave self-esteem + respect to the war veterans, regardless of their skin color

However most african americans were in the South (2/3)

  • Most lived in poverty + segregation
  • Regrouped to prepare the civil rights movement of the 50s

Federal Housing Administration

  • Discrimination for housing
  • Resulted in black ghettos (ex: South Chicago)
    • Riots from the whites that couldn't afford to move as the black population became massive

1.9 Hispanic Americans

  • They had good positions in the army, 11 won the Congressional Medal of Honour
  • Followed the african americans to the North to get work
    • This led to a shortage in farm workforce
    • Led to the importation of workers from Mexico (braceros)
      • They were exploited quite ruthlessly
  • They suffered from similar discrimination as the african americans
    • They were attacked by marines on leave in Southern California

1.10 Asian Americans

Major violation of civil liberties: internment of Japanese Americans

  • 120 000 of them sent to internment camps in 1942
  • Relocation was "necessary" to allow "security" on the West Coast, as they could aid the enemy in case of attack
    • Later admitted that all of that was done to support anti-japanese prejudice, wartime hysteria and greed
  • Contrary to other minorities, no one helped them (not even Earl Warren)
  • "Individual rights could be sacrificed to military necessity"
  • As a consequence, many lost their homes and buisnesses (total estimated to $400 million)
  • Despite that, they were determined to show their worth and enrolled in the army
    • 442nd Regimental Combat Engineers was the most decorated unit in American military history
  • Restitution of goods took a long time
    • They were still receiving in 1988 - Reagan

We admit a wrong. Here we affirm our commitment as a nation to equal justice under the law.

  • Reagan
  • Japanese citizens did not linger much on these events: they wanted to move forward
    • They managed to get very good education and work

The Chinese on the other hand, were sympathised with as they were Allies

  • They were suffering from the Japanese cruelty
  • Many found job opportunities and managed to get out of their ghettos

We tend to think that wartime unity was very present

  • However long-established conflicts and tensions simmerd just beneath the surface
  • Although progress had been made, much was left

1.11 Native Americans

  • Worked in the army, talking the tribal dialect so that japanese couldn't understand
  • They became part of the society after the war (didn't return to their reserves)
