1 Southern Strategy

1.1 Method

  • Democrats once relied on the "Solid South" as a source of voters
  • This changed as Nixon campaigned for a lot of what the Southerners wanted
    • During his first term, he showed his support for them
    • Attempted to slow down the desegregation of schools
    • Went against other measures that favoured African Americans
      • Voting Rights Act
      • Housing Act
    • Didn't like Warren - replaced by more conservatives

1.2 Results

  • Worked well:
  • 1969: The South is no longer a solid Democrat area
  • 1972: Won every single southern state (reelection)

2 Watergate

2.1 Story

2.1.1 Causes

  • Nixon's desire to control everything
    • Attempted to make everything
  • Wanted to win an overwhelming victory for his second term
  • Nixon gained a lot of power: wanted to have all the agencies under his control
  • Overzealous subordinates who wanted their president to win

2.1.2 Timeline

  • Two reporters were important in the investigation
    • Power of media
  • Senate Investigation Committee played the major role in linking Nixon and the illegal acts that were committed against Watergate
  • Supreme Court was the one forcing Nixon to hand in the tapes from the White House

It doesn't really matter why or who ordered the Watergate break-in. What really matters is how we dealt with it after it happened, and that's were we made our fatal mistakes

I gave them a sword and they stuck it in me - Nixon

2.2 Impact on Politics

2.2.1 Increase in Congressional control

  • The president lost power, which was given to Congress, regarding the agencies and the executive
  • Establishing limits on election fundraising
  • Privacy Act (1974): allows people to consult the information kept about them in federal files
  • Ethics in Government Act: disclosure of finances by government officials
  • People's view on the president changed
    • "Presidential majesty seemed diminished"
    • One of the reasons for which Reagan was elected

2.2.2 Democrat & Republican Parties

  • On the short term, helped the Democratic party:
    • gained more influence in the Congress
    • Election of Carter
  • Long term: Republican party became more conservative, paved the way for the 1980s

3 Domestic policies

3.1 Ford

3.1.1 Nixon Pardon

  • Ford didn't know what to do with Nixon
  • Pardons him in 1974
    • Prevents trial and imprisonment

3.1.2 Economic policy

  • Tried to cut inflation
  • Cut Federal Spending
  • Raised interest rates
  • Failed to reform radically the economy lead partly to the economic recession of 1974
  • Unemployment rose and competition from other countries (Japan for ex.) was strong

3.2 Carter

  • Won because he was an outsider, untainted by the scandals in the White House
  • Tried to be a "new" president
  • However this revealed his inexperience
  • Lack of clear policy & optimism

3.2.1 Economic policy

  • Wanted to increase demand
  • Came in a time of severe recession because of the oil prices
    • Stagflation - inflation @13% in 1973
  • Nationwide movement against an interventionist and big-spending government
    • States used "Proposition 13" which allowed them to cut taxes by a lot
