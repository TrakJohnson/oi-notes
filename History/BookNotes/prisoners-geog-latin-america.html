1 History

  • Colonisation
  • Serfs and land owners kept inequalities alive
  • Everything on the coasts ⇒ less development since the start
    • Because of the jungle, mosquitoes, disease inland

Problems with colonization:

  • Diversity: Treaty that divided created Spanish & Portuguese regions in 1494
  • Killed much of the natives
  • The artificial borders mean conflict

19th century:

  • Arms race, authoritarian regimes

Wars between countries in the continent still have consequences today:

  • Bolivia refuses to sell its oil reserves to Chile after their war

Old problems:

  • Drug trade

The drug cartels have paramilitary wings which are as well armed as the forces of the state, often better paid, more motivated, and in several regions are regarded as a source of employment by some members of the public.

4 Brazil

  • Almost as big as the USA
  • Very bad infrastructure: transport, railroads, roads
    • 1/3 of the country is jungle
    • Ecological problems with carving out the rainforest
  • Amazon river is navigable somewhat, but its banks are not construtible
    • Limits the amount of land available
  • Thanks to the savanna → a lot of soya beans production
  • The capital was moved inland in order to reduce inequality and promote develpoment of poorer regions
  • Brazil lacks the good trade from the rivers in the South that pass in argentina

Resource rich:
