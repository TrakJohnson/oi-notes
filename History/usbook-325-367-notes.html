1 Context

  • WW2 brought the Americans out of the Great Depression
  • New Deal legacy + power of govt during war = a more interventionist govt
    • More power to the executive (president)

1.1 Achievements

1.1.1 Economic

  • Successfully reduced the army from 12M (1945) to 3M (1946) to 1.5M (1947)
    • While avoiding mass unemployment (stayed < 4%)
  • Despite Congress, some interventionist measures were passed:
    • Creation of the CEA (Council of Economic Advisors) which recommended govt action when it was necessary
    • Employment Act: gave more power to the govt over the federal economy
  • In 1947, half of the world's goods were made by the US
  • Mid-1950s: massive economic growth
    • Thanks to new tech + baby boom

1.1.2 Social

  • GI Bill: allowed rest to the GIs, offered them education, housing, farm loans, etc
    • Spent $20 billion
    • Helped 7.8M veterans
    • Stimulated the economy as soldiers had money to spend

1.2 Struggles

  • Has a great predecessor: hard to hold the level

1.2.1 Congress

  • Despite being mostly Democrat, the Congress was divided
    • Southern Democrats ("Dixiecrats") voted with the Republicans
    • ⇒ had a hard time getting legislation through
      • Ex: almost all of his first 21 social reforms were blocked by Congress
    • Even worse when the Congress had a Republican majority (1946-1948)
    • President vetoed 250 bills / Congress overrode 12 of these vetoes
  • Constant battle against Congress to fight for the rights FDR acquired
    • Ex: voted for limiting the presidency to two terms (22nd amendment)
  • On his reelection campaign, Truman called them the "do nothing Congress"

1.2.2 Economy

  • Fear of the Great Depression returning
  • Industry couldn't keep up with the growth in demand after the war
    • Lead to inflation (25% in 1945-46)
    • Congress worsened it with tax cuts
  • Attempt at making the OPA (Office of Price Administration)
    • Congress vetoed & amended everything, rendering it useless
1.2.2.1 Trade Unions
  • Because of the war industry, many workers joined unions
  • These unions organized strikes around 1946 (demanding wage rises because of inflation)
    • They were important for the Democrat Funds
    • However they were encouraging inflation and there was public hostility
  • When a big miner + railroad strike was organized, Truman panicked and asked for emergency powers
    • Congress refused them, averting a big crisis
  • Powers of unions were reduced with the Taft-Hartley act
  • However, during the Korean War, steel workers went on strike
    • Truman tried to seize the industry under Executive Order, but this failed and the strike continued
      • This made Truman appear powerless

2 Fair Deal

  • Second presidency (1948)
  • Built on the 21 point program he had presented in 1945
    • Increase in minimum wage
    • Repeal of the Taft-Hartley act (unions)
    • Expand social security
    • Setting up public works
    • Healthcare program me
  • All of these were interventionist measures, where the govt. helped the poor/disabled, controlled the economy, etc
    • Like the New Deal
    • They also all depended on taxes

2.1 Success

  • In rural areas, where farmers benefited from access to electricity and other measures
  • Solved the housing shortage (build hundreds of thousands of low income subsidized homes)
  • Created the National Science Foundation
    • For Cold War + Education

2.2 Failures

  • Failed to go beyond the New Deal
    • Healthcare and education measures didn't get pass in Congress
  • People saw this as communism (socialized medicine)
    • An attack at their "freedom"
  • Couldn't be like FDR and "control" Congress
  • People wanted to consolidate what FDR had done and not go further

2.3 Contribution to the New Deal legacy

  • Built up on the help for the poor / unemployed / without housing
  • Increased interventionism in the govt
  • Strengthened the New Deal (social security especially)
  • Attempted to take the New Deal even further

3 McCarthyism

Red Scare - Fear of Communists

3.1 Source

Combination of fear of:

  • Govt. interventionism
  • Cold War tensions
  • Have to blame someone for something

3.2 Content

  • Impact on the American people:
    • The National Security Act + National Security council etc showed that there were weaknesses
  • Impact on Federal jobs:
    • Thanks to the Federal Loyalty Boards, more than 3k federal employees lost their job (1947 → 1951) because they were thought to not be "loyal"
    • States followed with their own similar laws
  • HUAC (House Un-American Activities Committee) went around to find people who were communists or connected to the communist party
    • Ex: investigation in Hollywood (1947 → 1951) where Arthur Miller was fined $500 and almost went to prison
  • First Amendment right can be cut off if its a National Security Concern:
    • People weren't protected by it as they thought
    • Or by the 5th amendment either
  • People named other people who named other people etc.
    • No one was spared: people were sentenced to death (ex: Rosenbergs in Los Alamos)
  • Verona project: counter-intelligence
  • McCarren Security Act: lots of measures against suspected communist
    • Govt can detain anyone they want in a national emergency (!)
    • Truman thought they had gone too far, but it was too late and Congress passed it
  • McCarthy took the hysteria to another level
    • Accused everyone - Republicans wanted to show through him that they were tough on Communist issues
    • Democrats had to do the same to not lose popular support
    • Skilled politician and manipulator of the media
      • But he above all had an audience
  • Communists in the US were seen as a source of the "failure" in Korea
  • Support of the Church as Communists were atheists
  • When Eisenhower came, he didn't do anything
    • Afraid ? Also didn't want to divide his party
    • Said that he would naturally fade away
  • Died down as McCarthy became too ambitious
    • Criticizing decorated soldiers
    • Finished censored

3.3 Impact

  • Hundreds lost their jobs
  • Companies went out of business
  • Anyone / any political party who was too extremist was "red"
  • Federal Govt. lost some of their best people
    • No one was left in the Asia desk → Vietnam failure
  • European Youth became anti-American
