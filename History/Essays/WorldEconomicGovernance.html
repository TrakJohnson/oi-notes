2 Bretton Woods System/Conference

Bretton Woods conference:

  • Create economics rules on the international level
  • Stabilise currencies and reduce big fluctuations
  • Prevent another Great Depression
  • Stay united vs communism (wasn't the USSR in there ?)

Keynes was there:

  • His goal was to have an "overseeing overlord" that would control the currencies
  • The IMF should regulate what's happening with the currencies

2.1 IMF

2.1.1 Keynes' dream

What Keynes wanted it to do:

  • Be able to regulate currencies itself
  • Fix currencies against the "bancor", something virtual that can't be bought or sold - just here to regulate

2.1.2 Role before

  • The exchange rate was fixed against the dollar, which itself was fixed against gold
  • This ensured some stability - but didn't last, as in 1971 Nixon abandoned the link between the dollar and gold
    • The IMF couldn't regulate anything anymore

2.1.3 Role now

  • Giving loans or financial support to states in need
  • "Monitors" the economy, by analyzing and giving forecasts
    • For example, said that Brexit would plunge the UK in economic recession
  • Gives advice on how to manage economy

The more you pay to the IMF, the more power you have - undemocratic ?

The IMF forces you to take measures after the money is given (SAPs)

  • cutting taxes
  • reducing spending
  • privatisation, etc

Imposes a view on economy that might not be the one the country wants to follow, however we can't just go on giving money away without consequences in the country

2.2 International Bank for Reconstruction and Development

1946 - now the World Bank

  • Provides a pool of investment for the middle income states

2.3 GATT

General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade

Created in 1947 - became WTO (World Trade Organization)

  • Used for trade deals and international trade rules
