3 How can it be measured ?

Money:

  • GDP: total values of goods and services produced by a country
  • GNP: Total value of goods and services including income generated abroad by industry based in home coury
  • GNI as GNP (most used now)
  • PPP (Purchasing Power Parity): how much you can buy

However:

  • Averages do not include inequality
  • There are other factors to be considered (quality of life, etc)

Other factors:

  • social cohesion
  • good governance (? how do you judge)
  • safery and security
  • health
  • employment
  • sustainability (air quality / env. quality)
  • education
  • employment

Definition (World Development Report):

A better quality of life in world's poor countries calls for higher incomes - but it involves much more, it encompasses better education, higher standards of health and nutrition, less poverty, a cleaner envionnment, more quality of opportunity, greater individual freedom, and richer cultural life.

3.1 Raw Indicators

  • Education: % Literacy / # years of schooling
  • Health:
    • Life expectancy
    • Access to safe water
    • # of inhabitants per doctor
    • Infant mortality
    • Food consumption
    • Energy consumption
  • Equality:
    • Citizen rights
    • Gender equality (in work, education, etc)

3.2 Composite indicators

  • HDI: Human Development Index (Health Wealth and Education)
  • HPI: Human Poverty Index

4 Sustainable Development

Meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs

Sustainability types:

  • Economic
  • Environmental
  • Cultural

5 What do we mean by poverty ?

Absolute poverty

  • Fixed amount of money per day (~$1.80)

Relative poverty:

  • Less than half of the country's median income
  • More accurate per country
