1 Political Context

1.1 A Federal System

  • Power is shared between central govt. and the individual states
  • National Powers / Shared Powers / State powers
    • This gives diversity in between states
  • Comes from the 13 original colonies
    • Developed individually
  • Founding fathers found a compromise between differences in states and national unity
    • Federalism was that compromise
      • Issue of where the balance should be - more or less power to the states
      • Democrat - more central
      • Republican - more independence

1.2 Seperation of powers

  • Legislative
  • Executive
  • Judicial

Same in all 50 states

Each of the branches can check on the other branches

  • Supreme court is politicaly neutral - normally

1.3 An entrenched constitution

  • Have to make sure that the political system's foundation will stand the test of time
  • To change the Constitution: need a lot of votes (2/3 of Senate & 3/4 of the states)
  • Consequence: Amended 27 times - 10 of them were done just after (Bill of Rights)
    • Comparaison: 24 in 50 years
  • Problem: it is quite vague, can be interpreted in several different ways
    • Ex: 2nd amendment (gun control)
    • An amendment can be used to do multiple things - wide range

2 The two-party system

Not intended by the founding fathers - no mention of parties

  • However they have been there since the beginning
  • Only Democrats and Republicans have been elected to president

Why two-party system ?

  • Second place get nothing in the House of Representatives

5 Key points of change - From consensus to schism

5.1 Civil Rights Movement - LBJ's "Great Society"

  • War on poverty - more social services
  • Democrats becoming the party of social liberalism
    • Creates opposition (Texas went from democrat to republican)
      • The "Solid South" in the Bible belt

  • Republicans shifted from removing slavery to going against Civil Rights

5.2 Reaganomics in 70s - 80s

    • End of the "big government" => tax cut, New Deal comes to an end
