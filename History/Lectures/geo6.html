1 What is globalisation ?

The process of creating networks of connections amongs actors at multi-continental distances, mediated through a variety of flows including:

  • People,
  • Information and ideas
  • Capital
  • Goods and services

What does it imply ?

  • Increased mobility and integration of goods, services, labor, tech
  • Compression of the world

1.1 Studying global systems

  • Economic, political, social and environmental interdependence
  • Unequal flows ⇒ stability & growth but also inequalities & conflicts
  • Unequal power relations:
    • Countries converging but population diverging

1.2 Measuring globalization

KOF Index:

  • Economic: trade, FDI, tariffs, etc
  • Social: tourism, telecom, immigration, media, internet
  • Political: international orgs, participation in UN, embassies

It has been rising since 1990s, and leveled off after the crisis.
Most globalized:

  • France and other European countries

2 What caused globalization ?

2.1 Transportation

Transportation has improved: people have more time, more money, travel more

  • Means improved: airplanes
  • FRICTION OF DISTANCE is reduced: transport costs are a small factor now

Containerization: standardization of the containers

  • Much quicker and cheaper
  • Allows for ULCVs (Ultra large container vessels)

2.2 Technology

  • Computers & Internet are everywhere
  • Communication, but also flows of capital has vastly increased
  • Increases a lot the cultural/social globalization → more and more connections, but then domination of certain cultures (inequality)

2.3 Improved mobility of capital / Reduced tariff barriers

  • Reduction of capital barriers (tariffs have been removed)
  • Remittances: money sent back to the country
  • FDI: Foreign Direct Investment
  • Reduced barriers → more integrated → more integrated
    • GATT then WTO
    • Protecionism went down after WW2 - going back somewhat
  • Economic trading zones: EEC, ASEAN, NAFTA, MERCOSUR
  • *Multinational companies are increasing in number
    • Companies are outsourcing (China for production, Indian services in telecom, etc)
    • Production has become globalized

2.4 Glocalisation

Global + Local:

  • Core brand image is adpated to the local market
  • McDonalds has custom menu for each country

2.5 Increased mobility of people

  • Tourism
  • Migration
    • a cause and a consequence of globalization
  • Socio-economic migrants
  • Refugee / Asylum seekers: special status for people fleeing persecution

3 Issues

  • Growing inequality
  • Globalization usually isn't compatible with sustainable development → lots of damage cause
  • Cultural homogenisation: cultural imperialism ?
  • Globalization limits countries' sovereignty
  • Risks are much more important in a globalized world
