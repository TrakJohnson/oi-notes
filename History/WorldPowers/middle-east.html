2.3 Middle East from 1914 to 1948

2.3.1 The Impact of WW1

  • Importance of oil, influence of Europe in the Middle East
    • Especially after WW1: strategic importance
  • Sir Mark Sykes - François George Picot
    • Negotiations into the Sykes Picot agreement
    • Before the end of the war (1916), split up the Middle East in case of Triple Entente win
      • France takes Syria and Lebanon
      • The Ottoman Empire becomes Turkey
      • UK gets Iraq, Jordan
  • 1932: creation of Saudi Arabia (Ibn Saud)
    • Population that had felt betrayed by the West - didn't get their part of the Ottoman Empire ⇒ rise in nationalism
  • British supported moderate Arabs, agreed to Iraq (1932) and Egypt's (1936) independence
  • 1944: Aramco (Arab American Company)
    • US agreed to give military assistance to Saudi Arabia in exchange for oil
  • Everyone wants oil: 7 sisters cartel

2.3.2 Middle East WW2

  • 1939: British White paper, no 2 states: all in one state
  • Defense of Suez Canal done by the English
    • Important for oil
  • Balfour decleration: creation of a "national home" (not a Jewish state !)
    • Churchill: says he can't do anything about it
  • Very high Jewish immigration during WW2:
    • Jews: 83k (1922) → 528k (1944)
  • Rebellions lead to the creation of the Hagana (Jewish Defense Force)

2.3.3 After WW2

  • 1947: A ship full of Jewish people was sent back to Germany
    • British govt gives responsibility to UN
    • Resolution 181: divide Palestine into two sides
  • May 14th, 1948: British leave and Ben Gurion (leader of the Jews) proclaims the creation of Israel

2.3.4 1st crisis: 1948 Wars

  • Arab states attack in response to the creation of Israel
  • Israels vs Egypt, Syria, Lebanon, Jordan (Arab Legion)
  • First Palestinian refugees: the displaced Palestinians
  • After the war, Israel: 55% of the British mandate to 79%
  • Armistice but no peace treaty, no plan to handle refugees

2.4 Midle East after 1948

2.4.1 Alliances

  • US supports Israel, Saudi Arabia, Iran
  • USSR supports Arab nationalism (to oppose itself to the US)

2.4.2 2nd crisis: Suez Canal Crisis

  • Frustration growing in Egypt, with the British troops still present
  • Nasser: did a coup and overthrew the royal Egyptian
    • He wanted to be independent and neutral

1955: Baghdad Pact, British ally with Turkey and Iran

  • Nasser is unhappy ! Doesn't want any British rule in the region
  • Doesn't want Western imperialism

Nasser uses "Voices of the Arabs", radio, to prevent other Arab countries from joining the Pact

February 1955: Israel invades Gaza and heads to Egypt because they have been backing Palestinian attacks

September 1955: Arms deal with Czechoslovakia, he breaks his neutrality policy (and recognizes communist China later)

  • Surprise, no one sees that coming

July 1956: Response from the British and US: cancel the loans they were giving to build the Aswan dam

Response: Nasser wants to nationalize the Suez canal

Response:

  • 29th of October, Israel invades Egypt
  • British and French bomb Egyptian airfields
  • US backed UN peace treaty force them to get out of the zone
    • USSR supported this decision, wanted to avoid a global nuclear war

2.4.3 3rd crisis: 1967 War (6 day war)

  • Israel is ally of US
    • Military aid
  • June 5th, 1967: Israel declares war to everyone
    • 6 days after, Jerusalem + West Bank (Water, Jordan river) + Golden Heights (Syrian artillery) + Gaza strips + Sinaï (buffer between them and Egypt) are in Israeli hand
    • Death ratio: 15:1 death ratio
    • Much better equipement and training for Israelis
  • Jewish settlements are going to be built
  • Outocome:
    • Many refugees, crisis not solved
    • New buffer zones for Israel

US sending $1B each year

2.4.4 PLO

Palestinian Liberation Organization

  • Launched attacks in Israel
  • Not a govt controlled organization
  • Lead by Yasser Arafat
  • Black September: Jordan tried to get rid of it, they moved to Lebanon

2.4.5 4th crisis: Yom Kippur War

  • 1970: Nasser dies, new president: Sadat
    • Wants peace, wants US to force Israel for peace
    • Forces out USSR aid, to show US they aren't mean
    • Doesn't work, as US is in Vietnam
  • New Syrian leader: Assad
    • Wants to break the no peace / no war stalemate

October 6, 1973: attack on the holy Israeli day Yom Kippur

  • Attacked Sinaï + Golden heights
  • In the end, Israel wins, however Sadat gets the US to be more friendly to Arab countries

1978: Land for Peace:

  • Sadat & Israeli prime minister Begin
  • Under the supervision of Carter, Camp David Accords / Washington Agreements
  • Egypt recognizes Israel as a state: first Arab state to do so

Sadat gets assasinated in 1981

1982: Israel attacks Lebanon, to kill PLO & Palestinians

2.5 Pan-stuff

2.5.1 Pan-Arabism

  • Unity is not on religion / unity around culture, ethnicity, language
  • BA'ATH party: "unity, liberty, socialism"
  • Israel is a threat to the Arab nation
    • All the Arab states are against, but they aren't organized
  • Nasser was pro Pan-Arabic: points out disorganisation
    • Suez crisis: Arab victory
  • Creation of the UAR: failed attempt to unite arabs
  • Camp david accords weren't really arab accords
  • Arab countries sided with Iran (while Iran isn't Arab: no unity)
  • Arab League: bring together as independant states
    • 1949: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Irak, Jordan, Lebanon
    • Egypt is kicked out after Camp David accords

2.5.2 Pan-Islamism

  • Political action linked to the Quran
  • Provides identity and allegiance
  • Wahhabism, Muslim Brotherhood, Hamas, Hezbollah
  • 1979: Islamic revolution, big turn of events, instability
    • Gives hope to extremist groups
  • Afghanistan war: US supplied weapons to Afghan resistance, blah
  • Iranian hostage crisis, etc
