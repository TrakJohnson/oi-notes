2.1 Definition

Globalizatoin as a concept refers both to the compression of the world and the intensification of oconsciousness of the world as a whole.

Integration of Trade, Finance, People: connect freely, one global marketplace

  • Changing technology, international trade increases
  • Marketing: producing more, clothing changing every 6 weeks
    • Producing at cheap wages, consumption en masse
    • Disposable products, no more repairing

Compression of space: further, faster, cheaper

3 History

Starts with the growth of sea trade, then industrial revolution accelerates

  • After WW2 picks up very fast (affluent society)

NIDL: New International Division of Labor

  • Division of labor: specialization in the production, specific steps done by specific countries / companies / people
  • Spreads the production process

5 Effects

5.1 Contagion

  • Political, Econmic and Social events spread rapidly
    • Financial crises in late 1990s + 2008
    • Arab revolts

5.2 Constraint

  • Put limits on what states can do - decrease sovereignty ?
  • Capital mobility increase: can move from state to state
    • States want to prevent capital flight - people and money

5.3 Impact on democracy

  • Democratically elected leaders are constrained by organization organizations and important world powers
    • Voters can't control what their state does
  • Democracy spreads faster - international standard of political legitimacy

6 Dimensions of Globalization

6.1 Political

  • UN to develop cooperation, local: EU
  • Key meetings: G8 (8 largest economies), G20, etc. → political organization, dealing with economic issues
  • International relations and comparative politics - everything is intertwined
  • Transnational issues: require state cooperation
  • Increase in political cooperation, increase of the influence of global politics on local politics

6.2 Economic

  • Trade, investment, migration, capital flows increase
  • Movements of goods, money and people
  • WTO (inherited from GATT), most important
  • Regional trading blocks: NAFTA, MERCOSUR, ASEAN
  • neo-liberal conomics: eradicating barriers to trade
  • Increased interdependence: 2008 crisis for example affects everyone

6.2.1 Why has trade accelerated ?

  • Peace
  • GATT / WTO: tariffs are 1/10th of those in 1947
  • Technical, technological and structural changes ("transport & communication revolution")
  • 80s: liberalization of the economy, Reagan/Thatcher
  • 1990s: integration of the USSR
  • Opening up of China & India

6.2.2 Consequences

  • Increased high competition, leads to very low prices ⇒ disadvantageous for less developed countries

6.3 Social / cultural

  • Western influence: Westernization or Americanizatoin
  • Islam influence
  • Asiatic influence
  • Eroding local traditions and languages ? but also creates diversity by exposing people to ideas from around the world
  • Promoting progress ?

Media & advertising → spreading culture, lifestyle

  • Globalization is mainly spread through consumer culture

6.4 Linguistic

  • English has become the main language

6.5 Demographics

  • Movement of people across borders

7 Key Drivers/Actors

7.1 The Contributors

  • Technology - cost of communication has fallen considerably
  • Liberalization of international trade - less protectionist methods (less tariffs, quotas, duties)
  • Deregulation especially with foreign firms
  • Language - English is considered the official language of many companies

TNC (TransNationalCompanies): key actor Positive impacts:

  • Investment and employment are increased
  • Tax revenues → goes to government
  • Importing tech, techniques and skills
  • Brings competition → better prices and choices
  • Increases balance of payment → reduce imports, increase exports

Negative impacts:

  • Increased competition: domestic firms aren't ready
  • Jobs created are low skilled
  • Env. impact: detrimental to local environment
  • Depletion of natural resources
  • Cultural and social impact → local cultures can be lost

FDI (Foreign Direct Investment): key to becoming a globalized country (from countries and TNCs)

  • Increasing integration of the world
  • Who really benefits ?

7.2 More Developed Countries

  • Get a better impact → more developped products
  • Products made in different countries

7.3 Developpping countries

  • Job creation in the industry

7.4 Obama speech

2008 speech in Flint

  • Competition is not local anymore
  • We can't turn back the clock
  • Instead of closing in, we must embrace the future and compete

8 Case Studies

8.1 Nike

  • Selling products throughout the world to increase their client base
  • Producing products all around the world
    • Better access to resources such as rubber
    • 500+ factories allow for better availability → reduce transport prices
    • Manufacturing in the right countries that have low wages and less worker protection
    • Highest revenue is in the US, yet there are no factories there: client countries vs producing countries
  • World influence → most recognized logo for ex., advertisement with well known athletes
    • Marketing based
  • Disadvantages:
    • For workers: the sheer size of the company means it delegates its work to small contractors, so it doesn't control locally/directly the working conditions - although if it did, would they improve ?
  • Nike invests locally instead of being mobile and leaving countries for cheaper labour
    • Training locally, etc.

Controversies:

  • Accused of paying lower than Vietnam's minimal wage (that's really hard to do)
    • $0.20/h, $37 vs minimum $45 per month
  • Doesn't improve worker's conditions

8.2 Toyota

  • Created after seeing the popularity of the automobiles in US + Europe
  • JIT (Just In Time) production, Toyotism
    • Maximize profits by minimizing loss of time, material and money
    • 63 production sites throughout the world (43% located in Japan, diminishing each year)
      • Moving to low cost production countries
    • Spare parts come from SouthEast Asia mainly
    • Maximize efficiency → related to globalization, produce more and faster
      • 200 components suppliers
      • Outsourcing and suppliers throughout the world
      • $300B spent per year on parts from suppliers
  • Sales Japan = US, exports in all continents
    • Preferred in most countries of the world
  • more accessible to modest people → rising middle class

8.3 Nokia 1100

  • Best selling cellphone: 250 million sold
    • Nokia was the first mobile maker during the 1990s
  • Cheap and efficient phones: no fancy features

Originally production in Finland

  • Then moved to Germany, then Romania (cheap labour force)
  • Moved from Mexico + Europe to Asia: search for cheaper workforce
  • R&D Finland + US + Japan
  • Produces 900k mobile phones per day
    • In 2006, produced 11-12 phones per second
  • Aim: emerging markets, majority of phones are in India
  • Contributed in 1998-2007 to 1/3 to Finland's economic growth, 30% of country's spending on R&D
  • Nokia however has decreased in sales: doesn't really adapt to new technologies, delocalizations
  • Technology have an impact on development and globalization
  • Cultural impact: 2000s, Nokia was the cellphone, had 40% of the communication sector
  • 1998 → 2017 4 → 400 million cell phones in Africa
  • Nokia has a good env. impact, witha lot of collect points for used phones
    • Howeve a lot oflong distance transportations (disadvantages of a global product)
  • Same product, but local advertising

9 Global Cities

9.1 Key questions

  • What are the characteristics of a global city
  • How is it an actor in the world's economy ?

9.2 Measuring

  • ATKearny ranks global cities with the Global Cities Index
    • Business activity (30%): capital flow, market dynamics, major companies present
    • Human capital (30%): education levels
    • Information exchange (15%): access to information through internet, media
    • Cultural experience (15%): access to major sporting events, museums, and other expos
    • Political engagement (10%): political events, think tanks and embassies
  • 2015 results: New York, London, Paris
    • Highest potential city: San Francisco

9.3 London

Global city because driver (and not only product) of globalization

9.3.1 Economic influence

  • 5th city GDP
  • 20% of UK companies based there
  • Top 3 World Stock Exchange
  • Attractive city for FDIs and businesses

9.3.2 Political center

  • Center for conferences etc.
  • However Brexit could decrease the Kearny Index
  • Capital of the Commonwealth (is that important ?)

9.3.3 Soft Power

  • Touristic center (Buckingham Palace, Big Ben, British Museum)
    • 20M tourists per year
  • Hosts Fashion Week, music festivals, etc.
  • Mediatic influence, large number of newspapers
  • Highest proportion of Internet users (91%) in the EU

9.3.4 Human capital

  • Strong education system: top schools (UCL, LSE, Imperial)
    • 100k international students from 200 nations (> US)
  • High diversity of population: Food, 300+ languages spoken in London
    • 41% foreign born people for Inner London's population
    • cosmopolitan city, diasporas from all the old colonies and more

9.3.5 Flows of Prosperity

  • Heathrow airport: links to the world
  • Olympics, global event, attracts tourists and investors
  • East London transformed successively by waves of immigrants: Huguenots, Jews, etc.
    • Poor part of London
  • Flows of people slowly integrating
  • Gentrification spreads as places become fashionable, attract shops and industries, banks etc.
    • People brought back in the city center
  • Companies are guiding people around, and government can also intervene to help a particular section of the city

9.3.6 Transport: Keep moving

  • International events improve local sectors, such as the London transports
  • Dependence on foreign business → importance of having a single hub for airport
  • Trading travelling within the UK for destinations outside - tourist or business

9.3.7 Challenges

  • Has to invest in large infrastructure projects
  • Social inequalities remain (27% poverty)
  • Brexit poses a risk to its political, and maybe economic world recognition
  • Cities consume 75% of the world's energy, env. challenge
  • Housing challenges, pricing increases

9.3.8 Districts

  • Business districts: Bridge Quarter, City (oldest), Central Business District (Shard Tower), Rail connection to Hearthrow

10 Presentations - Effects of Globalization

10.1 Positive

  • Interdependence between nations has several benefits
  • Socio-cultural benefits:
    • Education improves, possibility to study overseas
    • Technology spreads and develops much faster, improvements in communication
    • Cultural spread: people are influenced by other cultures, people mixing and coming together
      • More minorities, more diverse cities
      • Worldwide sporting events: World cup, Olympic games, etc.
      • Worldwide NGOs
  • Political scale
    • Cooperation between countries allows for stability and world peace
    • Sanctions for example for human rights are decided on the interstate level
    • International aid, financial support, organizations such as the EU
  • Economic scale
    • Increase in competition, and therefore prices go down and quality (somewhat ?) goes up
    • Markets are more efficient, have to optimize every step of the production for mass production
    • Rise of the middle class, even though globalization does not benefit to anyone
    • Access to raw materials and cheap labour is much easier to obtain
    • Investments (foreign for example)
  • Bridges gaps and inequalities, migratory flows connect people and enhance diversity
  • Who benefits ? Industrialized countries as they are the major actor, that exploit cheap labour and materials

10.2 Negative

10.2.1 Political

  • Migration ⇒ Increase in terrorism (???)
  • Job insecurity: not sure how this is political ?
    • Having a degree doesn't guarantee a job, increase of competition, looking for cheaper labor
  • CO2 emissions (how is this political ? I do not know)
  • Quicker use of finite resources → Coal, or petroleum
  • TNCs impact political globalization: very heavy lobbying, have significant influence on lawmaking, escaping taxes, etc.

10.2.2 Economic

  • Price fluctuation
  • Currency fluctuation: impact of the dollar on the rest for example
    • ex: 2008 crisis had a global impact
  • Unemployment: cheap labour outside the country means more qualified but higher cost workforce gets less jobs
    • Technology is also starting to replace small, cheap jobs

10.2.3 Social

  • Unhealthy lifestyles
  • Loss of culture, of identity, homogenization, Americanization
  • Migration can lead to problems withing a country: exploitation, hard adaptation, conflict within population
    • Brain drain too
  • Exploitation of workers
  • Inequalities, globalization benefits the rich
