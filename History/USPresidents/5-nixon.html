3 Nixon

3.1 Background

  • Had been Eisenhower's vice president
  • Lost against JFK in 1960
  • Not a very popular man
  • Won very narrowly in 68 / Nixon vs Humphey

1968: Majority of Democrats in both places

Wanted the return of traditional values, Law & Order, and the end of the war

  • Appealed to the silent majority

1972: Appealed to the Southerners

  • Slowing down Johnson's legislation - especially civil rights
  • Called the Southern Strategy

3.2 Law and Order

Deescalation in the Vietnam War

Used the CIA and the IRS (Internal Revenue System / FISC) to harass people

  • Create an enemy list
  • Included liberals and adversaries

3.3 New Federalism

  • Decrease the power of the federal government and give more power to the States
  • Revenue sharing: Federal government just gives money to the states and state decides what they fund
  • Despite this, he did invest in social aid:
    • Social security
    • Medicare / Medicaid
  • However he stopped some of LBJ's measures (housing, Job Corps)
    • Courts forced him to give back the money for the houses though

3.4 Stagflation

GDP down ⇒ Unemployment up ⇒ corporate earnings down ⇒ inflation up

  • 1970: Inflation & unemployment were at 6%
    • Trade competition from overseas / globalization
    • Too many people (baby boomers were at working age)
  • Money spent on Vietnam war and Space programs
    • Deficit spending rising
  • OIL CRISIS: 1973
    • Totally dependent on the Middle East
    • Yon Kippur war
    • October 1973: Countries part of OPEC (Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries)
      • Embargo ! (everyone who's with the US)
      • Great damage on the economy
      • March 74: started selling oil again, but the price had quadrupled
  • Second small one in 1979: Iranian revolutoin
  • He was forced to use government spending to fight that
    • Price freezes to limit inflation

3.5 Nixonomics

  • Deficit spending to stimulate the economy
  • Price freezing
  • 1971: New Economic Policy
    • Wage and Price Freeze
    • Tax cuts
    • Cuts the "gold window" preventing nations from demanding American gold in exchange for dollars
    • 10% Import tax to improve balance of trade
