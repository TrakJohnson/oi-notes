2 Interpreter of Maladies

Title: Mr Kapasi works as an interpreter for a doctor

2.1 Characters

2.1.1 Mr Kapasi

  • Has 2 jobs
  • lost hope
    • used to know many languages; never accomplished his dream

2.1.2 Mrs Das

  • American tourists
  • Only interested person in Kapasi's life
  • Selfish an uncaring parent
  • Very young parents - quite irresponsible

2.1.3 Mr Das

  • Interested in India
  • Very focused on India + the guide: tourist stereotype

2.1.4 Children

  • Quite young children
  • Illegitimate child (Ronny)
  • Children have more responsibility than the parents
    • "Bobby make sure that your brother doesn't do anything stupid"

2.2 Mrs Das + Mr Kapasi's relationship

  • they develop a relationship
  • Mr Kapasi has hope that Mrs Das will send him mail
    • Hope vanishes as the slip of paper flies away
  • Mr Kapasi thinks his job is worthless
    • Mrs Das has an interest in his job
  • Mrs Das says only to Kapasi that she had an illegitimate shild

2.3 Themes

2.3.1 Identity

  • Mr Kapasi feels worthless / useless, especially in contrast with his initial dream of being an important translator
  • Mrs Das changes this POV on his identity –> Mr Kapasi is an important person / more important than the doctor ?
  • Did that job to save his son - shows his selflessness
  • Very educated man; sacrificed all to save his son

All of his hope of a "Renaissance" flew away with this piece of paper - fragility of the relationship

2.3.2 Communication

  • Lost all of his skills in languages: deterioration of communication with time
  • No communication between mom and Dad

2.3.3 Contrast between India and America

  • Look like Indians, but…
    • Signs of US / And tourism
    • Gums; clothing
    • Takes pictures of everything
    • Relies on his tour guide more on the local people
  • Mr Das: knows all about India through the tour book
  • Mr Kapasi: knows all about US through Dallas show
  • Kapasi is shocked by her infidelity

2.3.4 Parenting

  • Parents fight for nothing - like children
    • Irresponsible/immature parents
  • Weird relation with childre: calls mother by first name

2.3.5 Concealment

  • Mr Das and Mrs Das do not talk / interact
  • Mrs Das has sunglasses - can't see through
  • "kept it a secret for 8 whole years"

2.3.6 Marriage Love

  • Mrs Das and Mr Das - Mrs Kapasi and Mr Kapasi
    • They all have problems

2.4 Symbols

  • Puffed rice
    • Mrs Das is careless, doesn't take care of what she does
    • "not offering puffed rice to anyone"
      • shows her selfishness
    • However drops it
  • Monkeys
    • embody a less; mrs das is punished for having an illegitimate child
    • Symbolize India's culture
  • Sunglasses
    • Protection from everyone else
    • Only takes them off when talking to Kapasi –> Shows intimacy
  • Camera
    • like sunglasses, covers him from the rest of the world
    • doesn't take care of what he's taking a picture of
      • starving peasant
    • doesn't really enjoy the trip - just takes pictures
  • Clothes/Colors
    • Tina's dress - reminds of a princess
    • Contrasts with the Indian people
      • "narrow brown shoulders"
    • Mrs Das' strawberry → young / passion; appetite for food, but also passiong through her affair
    • Clothes also represent how tidy Mr Kapasi is depsite his poverty
