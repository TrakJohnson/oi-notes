1 Myrtle's Death

  • Before the death
    • Sense of sickness
    • Fiery and provocative
      • "Beat me!" he heard her cry. "Throw me down and beat me, you dirty little

coward!"

  • Cars are a symbol of wealth and status, but also of industrial America in general
    • Wilson makes a living thanks to cars: irony as a car killed his wife
    • Sacrifices must be made in order to progress
    • Car accidents are common at that time: shows the precarious status of progress
    • Myrtle hwas therefore killed in her attempt to climb up the social ranks - she had too much ambition
  • Myrtle based her assumptions on the appearance of the cars, and who she thought was inside => relates to how we are easily fooled by appearances in The Great Gatsby.

Well, first Daisy turned away from the woman toward the other car, and then she lost her nerve and turned back.

  • Nervous situation, one small decisions can change everything

The "death car" as the newspapers called it, didn't stop; it came out of the gathering darkness, wavered tragically for a moment, and then disappeared around the next bend

Newspapers:

  • The fact that the car accidents appear there shows the importance of the cars
    • Only wealthy people have cars

Symbolic death:

  • Her dream dies
  • Irony as Nick is saying she "choked" while he is rich and she is of working class

Michaelis and this man reached her first, but when they had torn open her shirtwaist, still damp with perspiration, they saw that her left breast was swinging loose like a flap, and there was no need to listen for the heart beneath. The mouth was wide open and ripped at the corners, as though she had choked a little in giving up the tremendous vitality she had stored so long.

  • Quite a horrible description, but Nick doesn't seem to have many concerns about this
    • Shows the lack of respect from the higher class

1.1 Gatsby's & Wilson's death

The Death of a Dream - literally

  • Killed by George Wilson because he found out the car was his

Once he stopped and shifted it a little, and the chauffeur asked him if he needed help, but he shook his head and in a moment disappeared among the yellowing trees.

  • Gatsby wants to be left alone: shows the mystery surrounding the murder

Gatsby is disillusioned / melancholic

He must have looked up at an unfamiliar sky through frightening leaves and shivered as he found what a grotesque thing a rose is and how raw the sunlight was upon the scarcely created grass.

Water is the obstacle / water is where he dies

2 Other accidents

The girl who was with him got into the papers, too, because her arm was broken — she was one of the chambermaids in the Santa Barbara Hotel.

  • Accidents reveal the hidden side of society: no one would've know about the chambermaid if there had been no accident
  • The chambermaid's arm was broken, showing that the accidents created by the rich have consequences on the poor => same for Myrtle
