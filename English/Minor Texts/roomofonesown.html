2 Beginning

Definitions of Women and Fiction

  • Women and what they are like
  • Women and the fiction they write
  • Fiction that is written about women
  • 2 and 3 seem more convincing ? not sure how 1 relates to fiction

Tone of voice

  • Condescending/somewhat critical (?) tones: a little mocking (a reference, a respectuful allusion, some witticisms)
  • Not a lot of interest in that matter

Persuasive devices in the first page

  • She's showing her train of though: legitimacy of her opinion, she has made an attempt and isn't just giving out truths
    • Effort to give a complex and complete answer
  • Admitting mistakes, being honest → task is impossible

Mix of fiction and facts

  • Fiction reveals more than giving out facts
  • Truth can be found in fiction … just like lies can be found in "facts"
    • Challenging, questioning what is taken as authority
    • Chapter 2, most of things written about women are written by men, in power and want to keep their position of power
    • Irony, don't trust me just because I'm standing here as a lecturer
  • Women don't have a public voice → references to the outbursts in the women literature in their novels
  • There is no factual truth, or at least it cannot be said directly
    • Fiction suggests rather than tells directly

I should never be able to come to a conclusion. I should never be able […] to hand you after an hour's discourse a nugget of pure truth

  • Fiction is here more accurate than non-fiction

Lies will flow from my lips, but there may perhaps be some truth mixed up with them; it is for you to seek out this truth and to decide whether any part of it is worth keeping.

  • Gives a sense of responsibility / importance to the listener, who is the one who ultimately decides what is the truth

3 Luncheon scenes

3.1 Men's dinner

  • Challenging convention: describing the food as a still life
    • Why a painting and not a
  • Abundance, opulence
  • Optimism: we are going to heaven, etc.
  • Continuous, chains of long sentences
  • Link with Gatsby's parties: like a show, spectacle
  • Light in the spin glows
  • Ending: "one sunk among the cushions of the window-seat"
    • Contrasts with the ending of the women, where it brutally ends and everyone exits
    • Never ends vs brutal end
    • Pleasant place vs functional place
    • Window-seat: see the world, nature, inspiration, etc.

3.2 Women's dinner

  • Short phrases, cut out
  • October vs Spring, ingredients reflect the lack of privileges: "transparent liquid", "muddy market"
  • repetition of plain: no intricate, complex dinners
  • disappointment: "That was all. The meal was over."
  • "The light in the spine does not light on "
    • Contrast with the lamp bulb: initial thought vs something that grows, that's softer, that's not immediate but a product of "rational intercourse"
  • It is the university that create a hostile environment for women, and restricts their freedom
    • Which is then used as an argument to say that women are incompetent
  • Coal vs Light bulb: changing age, era ?
  • You should be grateful for what you got, how can you compain about that ?
    • Irony with the coal miners
  • Food is a necessity, a function, vs something that inspires for

4.2 Passage 3

Division in 3 parts:

  • Rejection from the library
    • Broken in thought, fiction vs reality, immersion
      • Reflection is hampered by daily life
    • Image of the angel: white wings vs black gown, hypocriticality of Christianity
      • kindly yet deprecating, contrasts between app earnace and reality
    • Wave me back: some impression of not addressing a human
    • Contrast between manhood: low voice, Fellow of the College → capital letters
      • Vs ladies
  • Description of the church
    • Importance of tradition: venerable and calm
      • Tradition doesn't change:
    • Opposition to tradition
  • Description of the congregation
    • Creased and crushed shapes: worn out, people of the past
    • Aquarium, Sanctuary: artificial world, virtual machine, prison but also protects them and preserves them
    • Bubble away from the real world

→ Shows the narrator's isolation
