1 Background

1.1 Hierarchy

  • God is the creator: created the world from chaos
  • Everything hsa a specific place in the hierarchy
    • God, Angels, Humans, etc
    • The "Great chain of being"
  • Fire, Air, Water, Earth
  • Macocosm: Universe, Nature Sky
  • Microcosm: sub-hierarchy (King, Church, Princes, etc or within animals, birds)
    • Is a reflection of the Macrocosm
    • There is even one for the emotions (Choler, Tears, Blood, Phlegm)
      • If there is imbalance, sin will come
  • If order is not maintained, devils and demons can come in: sin
    • There is always someone above you you must obey
  • Link to the story:
    • Beginning, the boatswain commands
    • Prospero lost his place as king

Character's role and relation to Prospero

    • Reminds him of his past and his "torment"

Before the time be out ? No more. - Prospero

  • Poses threats to prevent him from thinking about freedom

If tho more murmur'st, I will rend an oak and peg thee in his knotty entrails till though has howled away twelve winters.

9 Act III Scene 1 - Women

How is the character of Miranda portrayed ? How is gender explored ?

9.1 Women's role

No, precious creature, I had rather crack my sinews, break my back, Than you should such dishonour undergo, While I sit lazy by.

  • It is the role of men to work, and it would be a "dishonour" for women to do so and men to stay idle
  • She doesn't understand society's roles

Admired miranda […] O most dear mistress […] Precious creature

  • She is portrayed as an object to be admired ("precious", "admired")
  • Not to be touched or hardened by work
  • Miranda = "to be wondered at" in latin
  • Described as perfect

Characters:

  • Miranda is very modest - says that she wants to be a servant
    • She goes from Prospero's influence to Ferdinand's influence
    • Like Caliban from Prospero to Stephano
  • Ferdinand is shown as "bragging"

9.2 Miranda's ignorance

I do not know […] nor have I seen

  • Miranda's ignorance because of her confinement on the island

I am your wife, if you will marry me; If not, I'll die your maid

  • Blinded by the first person she sees - no critical thinking, total submission immediately

I would not wish Any companion in the world but you

  • The thing is, she doesn't know about any other suitable companions
  • Contrasts with Ferdinand, who is experienced
  • Knowledgeable men & ignorant women

9.3 Miranda's perfidiousness

My father is hard at study; pray now, rest yourself

  • Disobediance, thinking her father isn't present while he's on
  • Thinks she has some liberty
    • Which is but an illusion → ignorance

my father's precepts I therein do forget

  • Call to order, like a child who remembered what her parents told her
