1 Background

1.1 Hierarchy

  • God is the creator: created the world from chaos
  • Everything hsa a specific place in the hierarchy
    • God, Angels, Humans, etc
    • The "Great chain of being"
  • Fire, Air, Water, Earth
  • Macocosm: Universe, Nature Sky
  • Microcosm: sub-hierarchy (King, Church, Princes, etc or within animals, birds)
    • Is a reflection of the Macrocosm
    • There is even one for the emotions (Choler, Tears, Blood, Phlegm)
      • If there is imbalance, sin will come
  • If order is not maintained, devils and demons can come in: sin
    • There is always someone above you you must obey
  • Link to the story:
    • Beginning, the boatswain commands
    • Prospero lost his place as king

4 Act 1 Scene 2 - Power & Control

4.1 Propspero and his authority

4.1.1 Miranda

Character's role and relation to Prospero

  • Daughter
  • Used as a tool to regain political importance / appease his ennemies
    • to become the duke of Milan

Prospero's attitude and behaviour towards the character

  • Gentle / Kind / very peaceful relationship
    • Uses adjectives such as "wench"
  • Gives her his version of the facts
    • Doesn't insist on how he wasn't devoted to governing the city; puts all the blame on his brother

that a brother should be / so perfidious - Prospero

  • Extremely hypocritical: criticises their manipulativity but that's what's he's doing
    • She only sees one side
  • Manipulative: Molds her into what he wants: potrays himself fully as a victim of a conspiracy
  • Only tells her the story 12 years after
  • Choses to put her to sleep when he wants

Tho art inclined to sleep. 'Tis a good dullness, / And give it way; I know thou canst not choose - Prospero

4.1.2 Caliban

Character's role and relation to Prospero

  • Prospero's slave
  • They hate each other (athough this was different)

Prospero's attitude and behaviour towards the character

  • Extremely conflictual relationship

Thou poisonous slave, got by the devil himself / Upon thy wicked dam, come forth - Prospero

  • Two way hatred: the slave answers

You taught me language, and my profit on't / Is, I know how to curse. The red plague rid you / for learning me your language

  • Prospero's excuse for his master position is the attempted rape on his daughter
  • While Caliban argues he is the one who kicked him out from his island

4.1.3 Ariel

Character's role and relation to Prospero

  • Air spirit
  • Fulfills Prospero's request that involve magic → grants him his power

Prospero's attitude and behaviour towards the character

  • Is his propperty + gives him orders
    • "Approach, my Ariel. Come !" - insists on "my"
  • Flatters him to show "sympathy"

My brave spirit ! […] Why that's my spirit !

  • Although he gives the appearance of being sympathetic, he has no mercy for his servant

thy charge / Exactly is performed; but there's more work - Prospero

  • Refuses to grant him his liberty
    • Even if he promises, uses the argument that he freed him from Sycorax to keep him longer
    • Reminds him of his past and his "torment"

Before the time be out ? No more. - Prospero

  • Poses threats to prevent him from thinking about freedom

If tho more murmur'st, I will rend an oak and peg thee in his knotty entrails till though has howled away twelve winters.

  • Used as a tool for marriage and strategical union

Prospero's attitude and behaviour towards the character

  • Manipulation
    • Gives him the illusion of a magical, beautiful island
