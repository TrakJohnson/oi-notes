3.1 Work on both poems - Essay question

Vivid imagery and symbolism are inherent in the lyrical quality of good literature. With precise reference to two works you have studied, discuss how the writers use words to create images that touch and enlighten us

In Annie John and Seamus Heany's poems, the complexity of family relationships is explored through the theme of admiration and power, the sons and daughter's quest for identity, and how characters sometimes break the cycle, while others do not. In doing so, it revelals the evolution of characters and how they emerge from childhood.

  • Contrast between "savoured", "rich crash"
  • As if he was eating something → feasting off of unconventional things

A white face hovered over the bottom

  • Duplication, seperation from himself

Clean new music

  • Personification of the sound, transformation of reality

Extended metaphor of the wells

  • Personal development → coming of age

Plan:

  • Children's point of view, exploration, youth and innocence
  • Danger and growing up
  • Retrospection and art → fresh look on his childhood
    • Looking back on himself through the well, retrospection (goes down deeper)

Don't forget art: the title is about it ("personal helicon") The extended metaphor of the well:

  • Interaction with nature, but also
  • The well of the mind, memory

Step by step:

  • At first its very deep → "So deep you saw no reflection in it"
  • Then he starts to see things (shallow), but its shallow so less important
  • And then a new perspective ("a clean new music")
  • The wells show the step by step transition to adulthood, but also the act of retrospectionxxxx
