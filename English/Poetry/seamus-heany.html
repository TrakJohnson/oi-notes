4.1 Work on both poems - Essay question

Vivid imagery and symbolism are inherent in the lyrical quality of good literature. With precise reference to two works you have studied, discuss how the writers use words to create images that touch and enlighten us

In Annie John and Seamus Heany's poems, the complexity of family relationships is explored through the theme of admiration and power, the sons and daughter's quest for identity, and how characters sometimes break the cycle, while others do not. In doing so, it revelals the evolution of characters and how they emerge from childhood.

5 Mid-Term Break

Key themes:

  • Place of the narrator in society, role to play
  • Responsibility, growing up
  • Sense of community

Analysis

  • Solitude of the child facing adversity
    • He doesn't know how to react, what to do
    • Is confused about what is happening around him
    • His perception of the world has been altered
    • First experience of death: "meeting" his father crying, for the first time
    • Breaking safety, forced maturity
      • Contradiction between him waiting for parents to help him, while the parents can't do that
      • He's not at school, yet he's learning about grief → contrast with
      • People do not act like you'd expect them to
      • Sense of responsibility: he's the eldest
  • Innocence of the child (baby cooing) vs strangers are "whispering"
  • Small rural community: everyone is coming to pay their respects
    • Sense of cooperation in adversity

The first part is about how people are reacting:

  • Nothing about himself, he doesn't know how to react
  • He is between the baby and the old men
    • Baby reflects life that continues, innocence, incomprehension: enumeration of action verbs
    • Old men (father, Big Jim Evans) reflect strength and experience (taken funerals in his stride) yet
  • he's the "eldest", yet no feeling are reflected, no sense of understanding
    • conflict between his position as "old": they "shake my hand", "I was the eldest"
    • "whispers": secrecy, unease, respect; sense of community and communication
  • "Away at school" reflects his position, away from the family's mourning

Mother's description:

  • Mother is a link between her child and the events, the community: "held my hand"
  • "coughed angry tearless sighs" → another form of mourning, coughed, impression choke
    • Contrast with the traditional values, man crying and woman angry

Real matter is only evoked at the end:

  • Ambulance, corpse only mentioned in stanza 5
  • Mummifying, sacrilisation

Use of iambic pentameter sometimes, also several alliterations

"Counting bells knelling classes to a close:"

  • There's no coming back
  • Bells of funeral, foreshadowing: our neighbours come, exteriority

Vocabulary: clinical, impersonal

  • Transition from the public sphere to the personal one
  • "Next morning"
  • Use of precise time markers, sense of rehearsal, but also memory: he remembers every moment of it
    • Going on for ever: awkwardness
    • Shift in time in the last stanzas

Rhythm:

  • Iambic pentameter rare, random
  • "The baby cooed and laughed and rocked the pram" → iambic pentameter, tranquility, innocence, lullaby
  • Hyphens showing breaks in rhythm
  • rocked the pram // when I came in, and I was embarassed
    • when he comes in, breaks the rhythm: he has no place

Links:

  • Contrast between Myrtle's death scence, and the description of the corpse vs no gaudy scars
  • Annie John: getting out of innocence, the hand, fascination by funerals, step in learning

10 Sonnet V

Poem reflects his loving but contrasted relationship with his mother Sonnet ⇒ love I. A silent relationship revealed through family work

  • Silence, not a word spoken: only description of movement
    • "coming close, holding back"
  • Flow of actions that connect them: "and", "first", "and", "then"
  • Sensations: cool, damp → reflects the atmosphere
  • Contrast between the uniqueness of the moment: "split second"; and the banality of the action (as it is work) → "nothing had / that had not always happenned"
  • "dried-out" contrasts with "damp"
  • sail in the crosswind → voyage, mother and child embarked / contrast with the same imagery with the father ?
  • Reference to poverty (ripped-out flower sacks) enhances the importance of family

II. A symmetrical yet dissonant relationship

  • Symmetrical movement, image of a dance: "stretch and fold and end up hand to hand"; "day by day"; "touch and go"
  • x and o, not matching yet intertwined, marked on flower sacks
  • Rhyme and rhythm
    • Some of the rhymes work: them, hem
    • Yet other are incomplete: line, linen;
    • Others are completely dissonant: shook, thwack
    • Mimics the waves, symbolism of the ocean and the coming and going
      • references to the father ? Follower
      • iambic pentameter also varies, shows the oscillation of his memory between the soft to and fro, and the differences that remain
  • Contrast between the mother, hard working, "sewing" → while he sews
