3 The Poet in the Box

What image do we have of a poet ? Isolation: box, prison Possesed by poetry.

3.1 Activity 4 - Writer's effect's

Metaphor: There poetry was a grasshopper in the bowl of his hands

Poetry is compared to a grasshopper for multiple reasons. To start with, a grasshopper is usually rare and well hidden in its environnement. It is a very volatile insect, which is very hard to catch: this shows that poetry that Brandon is seeking is a very rare sight, which is the achievement of his efforts to be alone in the box. The "bowl of his hands" reinforces the importance of this moment, as the grasshopper could jump out at any moment and be gone for ever.

Similie: The scratching of his pencil like fingernails on dungeon stone.

"Fingernails on dungeon stone" evoke a feeling of disgust and reluctance, which contrasts with the scratching of a pencil. This difference links the poet to a lone prisonner in a dungeon, further enhancing his loneliness.

Similie: Like the script of a pharaoh's deeds on pyramid walls

This similie comparing the poetry to writings on a pyramid's walls, which gives the idea of a lost and unknown great work. This comparaison increases the importance of the poet and makes it an artifact, hidden from society, to discover.

Metaphor: Metaphor spilled from the light he trapped

This metaphor gives the reader the impression that the poet's eyes are filled with light, giving an unsual perpesctive on the simple act of looking. This suggests that the poet sees things differently: it is not him who sees the light, but the light that fills him. This isolates Brandon, but makes him a more "poetic" and mysterious figure, along with the poetry he creates, as if it was made of pure light.

Metaphor: Metaphor, that cackling spirit

This stylistic device tranforms the tools of poetry into an evil and possesive creature. The verb "cackling" is usually associated with a witch's laugh, and the description as a spirit

Metaphor: The cold was a blanket to him

Here the main intriguing feature to this metaphor is the opposition between the blanket and the cold, as these two words are usually considered as antonyms. This further highights the need for isolation of the poet, as Brandon seems to have different conceptions of things: what appears bad to us is for him a protection.
