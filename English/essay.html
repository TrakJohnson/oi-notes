2 Social Criticism

To what extent is social criticism inherent in good literature ?

Works used: Gatsby, Punishment, Wedding Day

Gatsby:

  • Division of Old Money / New Money → Tom Buchanan, superiority of the white race
  • Illegality, corrupted society → Wolfsheim
  • Valley of Ashes → cannot rise

Seamus Heany:

  • Punishment
  • Social criticism is linked to personal feelings

Thesis statement: Social criticism is recurrent theme in good literature, however it isn't sufficient on its own

I. Social criticism is inherent

  • Denounciation of social norms is a key component in works of literature
    • Punishmenent: denounciation of scapegoating, description
    • Wedding Day: demented bride, role of power between husband and wife
  • Contrast between classes and social constructs is a source of conflict and tension → leads to plot devices
    • Plot of the story, Gatsby's motivation, is the main plot yet is also a criticism of Old Money / New Money / inaccessbility
    • Characters embody a social problem: flappers with Jordan, depiction of a whole social movement through one person in the novel
    • Setting embodies a social problem → Valley of ashes, TJ Eckleburg
    • Tom Daisy vs Gatsby, Old vs New Money
      • Valley of Ashes, George Wilson & Myrtle → death of social values (Jordan), motif of the car

II. Social criticism does not have to be the central theme

  • Social criticism cannot hold on its own, and must be backed up with other, more personal themes
    • Seamus Heany criticises society's norms and requirements
      • Personal feelings of solitude, vulnerability, dementia
      • Before being a criticism of society, Wedding Day is a personal poem
  • Social criticism is a goal, but not a mean:
    • Social clashes explored through Gatsby's love of Daisy
  • Social criticism does not define our relation with characters
    • we have compassion for Gatsby and his love, despite the criticism, his
